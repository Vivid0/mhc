Our Story

The winery now known as Morgan Hill Cellars has seen ownership pass to three separate Italian families over the years. Though they faced many hardships the love of the business, the wines and the passion of its owners made the winery successful time and time again.

The year was 1913. Woodrow Wilson had just been sworn in as President of the United States. Congress had passed the 16th Amendment instituting a federal income tax. Turmoil in the Balkans foreshadowed the beginning of more terrible things to come. Suffragettes across the western world continued the struggle to grant women the right to vote. And the first ship would enter the Panama Canal.

History-shaping events were taking place all over the world, but in the quiet, little, California town of Morgan Hill, a man would begin a legacy that would last for over a century.

Most of the information below was gathered and arranged by author and wine enthusiast, Bev Stenehjem.

You can learn more about Morgan Hill Cellars and seven other historic wineries in her book, The Wineries of Santa Clara Valley.

Colombano

Camillo Colombano emigrated to the United States in the early 1900s from Piemonte in Northern Italy. He and his two brothers, Eligio and Pietro arrived at Ellis Island with Barbera root hidden in their shoes.

Initially finding work in Connecticut, Camillo eventually moved to San Francisco where he and his brothers worked delivering wine and brandy by horse and buggy.

Camillo was a man of impressive stature. Standing at 6’4”, his height along with his fair hair and blue eyes meant that he always stood out in the crowd.

Blazing a trail

Camillo began buying up land in Morgan Hill. By 1913, he owned 80 acres of rich, Santa Clara Valley soil. With Barbera root stock he’d brought over from Italy, he planted grapes and opened Colombano Winery that same year.

Pedrizzetti

Fortunato Pedrizzetti along with his wife Louisa and son John emigrated to the United States in 1903 from Taino in the Lombardy region of Italy. The family eventually settled in San Martin, California, just south of Morgan Hill.

John soon grew into a man and married Eda Moisso-Maggoria. Together, they had a son named Ed. Soon after Ed’s birth, John’s father, Fortunato, was killed in a farming accident.

The Rise of the Phoenix

John befriended a fellow immigrant by the name of Camillo Colombano. By the time Camillo was ready to retire from the wine business in 1945, John agreed to purchase the business from him. John changed the name to Pedrizzetti winery, expanded the range of varietals grown on the property and installed a truck scale in front of the first tank house. By 1968, John retired, passing the winery on to his son, Ed and daughter-in-law, Phyllis.

Ed and Phyllis worked together, using a divide-and-conquer strategy when it came to tackling the necessities of the wine business. Ed worked the vineyard, the bottling line and the delivery truck to fulfill orders while Phyllis designed the labels, managed the tasting room and went on to become the first female president of the Greater Santa Clara Valley Wine Growers Association.

Through the years, the Pedrizzettis faced several hardships, not the least of which was a devastating fire that destroyed a large portion of the winery in 1996. However, not the type to taking it lying down, Ed and Phyllis rebuilt and used the disaster as inspiration for a new name for the tasting room and a new label: La Fenice “The Phoenix”. Together Ed and Phyllis carried on for several more years finally selling the winery in 2006 and retiring to Montana.

Sampognaro

The Sampognaros left the island of Sicily in 1930 to seek a better life in the US. The family settled in New York at first, finding it the first place they stopped to be as good as any. There were five children including, Sebastiano.

After teaching himself to speak English, Sebastiano started a family and later inhertited a papier-mache business from his widowed sister which he then converted into a vaccum form plastics business.

He soon employed several people including his sons: Michael, Robert and Richard.

Sebastiano semi-retired in 1964 and, following his mother and sisters, moved to California.

New Beginnings

Michael “Mike” Sampognaro met Maryclaire McGarry while she and her family vacationed at a house in Long Island just next-door. The two were soon married and started a family of their own. Just before the birth of their third child, the couple decided to follow Mike’s father out to California.

When they first arrived in California, Michael followed in his father’s footsteps starting a plastics business manufacturing decorative displays, vacuum forms and packaging for electronic components. After that, he continued with the entrepreneurial tradition, starting several other business including a machine shop and tire center.

Meanwhile, Maryclaire found work at National Semiconductor, one of the companies that would give Silicon Valley its namesake. Mike soon followed her into the high tech world working first for Apple Computer and then moving on to work in the video streaming space.

After retiring from their high-tech carriers, Mike and Maryclaire purchased Pedrizzetti Winery in 2006. They look forward to carrying on the legacy of one of Santa Clara Valley’s oldest wineries.
